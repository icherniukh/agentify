## From Official BM Documentation

### 1. The 8 Placement Criteria (Official Names)

**Source:** [Criteria for Camp Placement & Good Standing](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)
(also at `/event/participate/camps/placement-process/criteria/`)

The official criteria page lists 8 evaluation areas. Criteria 1-3 apply **only to theme camps**; criteria 4-8 apply to **all placed camps**:

1. **Interactivity** (Radical Inclusion, Gifting, Communal Effort, Participation)
2. **Visual Impact** (Radical Self-expression)
3. **Uniqueness of Offering** (Radical Self-expression, Immediacy, Radical Inclusion)
4. **Good Neighbors and Citizens of BRC** (Radical Inclusion, Civic Responsibility, Communal Effort)
5. **Culture and Values** (Decommodification, Radical Self-reliance, Radical Inclusion)
6. **Safety, Protocols and Procedures** (Civic Responsibility)
7. **MOOP / Leave No Trace**
8. **Self-reliance / Strain on Resources** (Radical Self-reliance)

Each criterion has two subsections on the official page: "Here's What We Hope To See" and "Here's What We'll Look For."

### 2. Interactivity Is the Primary Factor

**Source:** [Completing the Placed Camp Questionnaire](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024039188-Completing-the-Placed-Camp-Questionnaire)

> "The interactivity that takes place within your theme camp is one of the most important factors when assessing your camp."

> "Take some time to think about what you will offer and write in concrete, descriptive language about the details. Be prepared with a schedule of days and times of events you will be hosting. Nebulous, esoteric, or incomplete descriptions leave us confused (and not in a good way)."

> "Placement considers how unique your interactivity is -- whether you are offering something that could only be done at Burning Man or doing something standard but in a new and wonderful way."

### 3. Interactivity Must Scale With Population

**Source:** [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988-Planning-a-Theme-Camp)

> "Theme camps: interactivity must increase proportionally with population."

The PCQ explicitly asks camps to break down how their total population contributes to interactivity (the "Campers and Interactivity" field, max 6000 chars, required for standard track).

### 4. First-Year Camp Size Cap

**Source:** [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988-Planning-a-Theme-Camp)

> "All new theme camps that have never been placed in Black Rock City before are capped at 100' x 100' or 10,000 square feet which generally holds up to 25 people."

> "If your theme camp has a successful first year with placement and is in good standing, you will be able to double your size."

### 5. Camp Lead Should Not Be a First-Time Burner

**Source:** [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988-Planning-a-Theme-Camp)

> "We strongly recommend that you not delegate this job to a first time Burner" (referring to the camp lead role).

Not a hard rule, but an official strong recommendation from the Placement team.

### 6. CAMP Advisor Recommended for First-Year Camps

**Source:** [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988-Planning-a-Theme-Camp)

> "We recommend a CAMP advisor in your first year." (Camp Advisory and Mentorship Program)

### 7. Night Lighting Matters for Prominent Placement

**Source:** [Camp Resource Guide](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/so-you-want-to-camp-at-burning-man/camp-resource-guide/)

> "If your camp wants placement on the Esplanade, Portal, Plaza, or Avenue, your camp needs a plan for night lighting."

> "All Esplanade, Portal, Plaza, and Avenue camps must have their frontages open and lit at night through Saturday night/Man Burn to serve as landmarks."

**Source:** 2026 Sample PCQ (Night Lighting field):
> "Well-lit camps may be placed in more prominent locations as navigation landmarks."

### 8. Frontage Must Draw People In

**Source:** [Camp Resource Guide](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/so-you-want-to-camp-at-burning-man/camp-resource-guide/)

> "When someone is walking or biking down the street, what will they see? How will people know you're a theme camp? What about your camp would compel them to get off their bike and come participate?"

### 9. Consent and Opt-In Are Explicit Requirements

**Source:** [Criteria for Camp Placement & Good Standing](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)

The criteria page states camps must provide "clear ways for participants to opt in to engage with your interactivity. No one should feel coerced into participating, and informed consent should be" at the core of activities. Camps must have "predetermined ways for participants to easily opt in (or out) of any activity."

### 10. Decommodification / No Transactional Exchanges

**Source:** [Decommodification, Gifting, Participation and PAYticipation](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/cultural-direction-setting-decommodification/commodification-gifting-decommodification-and-participation/)

> "Any camp member engaging in an on-playa activity, on behalf of or within the camp, that requires payment for those services is not permitted, unless it's via an approved Outside Services vendor."

> "Commodifying resources (packaging, advertising, or marketing camp space, tickets, an experience, etc.) and allowing financial contributions to substitute for participation is not only in opposition to Burning Man's values, but significantly damages the culture."

**Source:** [Commerce, Branding & Decommodification -- Survival Guide 2025](https://survival.burningman.org/rules-and-regulations/commerce-concessions/)

> "The sale of products and services by participants is strictly prohibited."

> "No cash transactions are permitted between burners."

### 11. MOOP: Specific Items Flagged

**Source:** [Matter Out of Place (MOOP)](https://burningman.org/event/preparation/leaving-no-trace/moop/)

Explicitly flagged MOOP-risk items: glitter (not allowed in BRC), feathers, rhinestones, artificial grass, sequins, synthetic wigs, styrofoam coolers, loose confetti, straw, hay bales.

"Micro-MOOP" category: shredded/broken debris under 0.5cm -- includes glitter, broken glass, fabric bits, paint chips, wood debris.

### 12. Post-Playa Report Required for Good Standing

**Source:** [Post-playa Report and Good Standing Status](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415060235796-Post-playa-Report-and-Good-Standing-Status)

> "Completing this report is required to remain in good standing with Placement and to be eligible for future placement in Black Rock City."

Standing is determined by reviewing: Post-playa Report, feedback from other BM departments, Neighborhood Interactivity Feedback form, Placement/PEERS notes, and MOOP/LNT reports from Playa Restoration.

### 13. Camp Standing Levels and Consequences

**Source:** [Criteria for Camp Placement & Good Standing](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)

Three levels: **good standing**, **limited standing**, **not in good standing**.

- Not in good standing: no Stewards Sale tickets, may not receive future placement.
- Limited standing: 25-75% of normal Stewards ticket allocation and/or reduced space.

Factors that damage standing:
- Not showing up or not using reserved space
- Not following through on interactivity/frontage per questionnaire
- Bad MOOP rating from Playa Restoration
- Not completing Post-playa Report
- Not being a good neighbor (sound policy violations, boundary disrespect)

### 14. Space Demand Exceeds Supply

**Source:** [A Turning Point for Placement (Burning Man Journal, 2019)](https://journal.burningman.org/2019/05/black-rock-city/building-brc/a-turning-point-for-placement/)

> In 2019, 1,914 PCQ submissions -- 24% increase from 2018. "For the first time, there wasn't enough room for everyone wishing to be placed."

**Source:** [Adapting to Growth and Limits (Burning Man Journal, 2024)](https://journal.burningman.org/2024/02/black-rock-city/building-brc/adapting-to-growth/)

> 2024 SOI: ~1,300 submissions from theme camps, including 152 new theme camps. In 2022-2023, ~90% of BRC was placed -- "truly hitting the maximum."

This means first-year camps are competing for limited remaining spots and must make a strong case.

### 15. Multiple Volunteers Review Each PCQ

**Source:** [About Us & Volunteering (Placement Team)](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/placement-team/)

> "Multiple volunteers on the Placement Team review every questionnaire."

Review happens March-April. Map creation and placement decisions happen in May.

### 16. Convenience Camping Prohibited

**Source:** [Eliminating Convenience Camping in Black Rock City](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/cultural-direction-setting-decommodification/convenience-camping/)

> "Convenience camping is not permitted in BRC, and runs totally counter to the values of the community."

Camp focus must be on public contribution, not camper comfort.

## From Community Knowledge (Forums, Blogs, Reddit)

### 1. ePlaya: Interactivity Is the Deciding Factor for Oversubscribed Placement

**Source:** [Placement Questionnaire Tips for 2016 -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=76853)

> "Since there are more theme camps requesting placement than available space, camps that are doing more interactive activities will get placed."

> "For larger camps, more interactivity is expected and needed."

> "Details about your interaction should be placed IN the interaction field, not elsewhere."

> "The interactivity considered for placement is activities happening within your camp space itself -- things like parades at your camp count, but events happening elsewhere on the playa do not count as camp interactivity."

### 2. ePlaya: Be Specific, Not Esoteric

**Source:** [Tips For A Successful Placement Questionnaire 2015 -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=73380)

> "Presume the placer knows nothing about your camp."

> "Be descriptive about what you're doing that year -- rather than saying 'we're bringing food,' specify details like 'we'll be making amazing yummy quesadillas.'"

> "Use enthusiasm in your questionnaire to show how excited you are about your camp's plans."

> "Pay attention to punctuation and spelling, and avoid using all caps."

### 3. ePlaya: Camp Lead Must Be Decision-Maker, Available by Contact

**Source:** [Tips For A Successful Placement Questionnaire 2015 -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=73380)

> "A key person who can answer questions and make decisions should fill it out, not the sparklepony newbie who doesn't have any answers."

> "That person needs to be available by email/phone from the time the questionnaire is submitted until placement is announced."

### 4. ePlaya: Layout Must Account for Everything

**Source:** [Tips For A Successful Placement Questionnaire 2015 -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=73380)

> "Mark what's the frontage on your map, account for all your space so you can contain your whole camp within the camp."

> "Make sure your plan has all your big things on it (domes, major structures, etc)."

> "Be realistic about your needs -- account for infrastructure, camp members tents, and parking all the vehicles."

### 5. ePlaya: First-Year Camps Must Justify Space With Public Service

**Source:** [Placement for 1st Year Camp -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=84057)

> "Space is limited -- camps need to justify the space reserved by offering a public service to other burners, not just for tents and cars."

### 6. ePlaya: Use Your Personality, Show Enthusiasm

**Source:** [Tips For A Successful Placement Questionnaire 2015 -- ePlaya](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=73380)

> "Be yourself and use your personality when answering questions -- if you're funny or sarcastic, let that come through."

### 7. Brad Templeton Blog: Premium Locations Go to High-Interaction Camps

**Source:** [Placing Camps at Burning Man -- Brad Ideas](https://ideas.4brad.com/placing-camps-burning-man)

> Premium locations "are valued in part for being close to things but mainly because they are high traffic for camps showing off interesting art or interactivity."

> "Far more camps want to be in the premium locations than there is room."

### 8. SIDESHOW Camp: Example of Successful Detailed Frontage/Interactivity

**Source:** [Camp Design, Layout and Features -- SIDESHOW 2017](https://sideshow2017.weebly.com/camp-design-layout-and-features.html)

This is a placed camp's public planning page showing the level of detail successful camps put into their frontage and interactivity planning: 80-100 ft frontage with hand-painted banner art, lit up at night, bike parking along frontage, multiple named interactive features with specific descriptions (BIG SHOW, Odditorium, Midway Games), daily scheduled events.

Useful as a benchmark for what "concrete, descriptive language" looks like in practice.

### 9. Wizard of Odds: Casino-Adjacent Activities Exist at BM

**Source:** [Burning Man 2022 (Part 2 -- Theme Camps) -- Wizard of Odds](https://wizardofodds.com/newsletter/september-22-2022/)

The author (a professional gambling mathematician) attended Burning Man 2022 and reviewed theme camps. He presented "a lecture on the math of casino games" at Math Camp. This confirms casino-themed educational/entertainment activities occur at BM without placement issues, though this was a lecture within an existing camp, not a standalone casino camp.

## Casino/Gambling-Specific Guidance

**No documented guidance found specific to casino-themed camps in official BM documentation.**

Specific findings from the research:

1. **No official BM policy mentions gambling, casino, betting, or wagering** -- either to prohibit or to permit them. The official criteria pages, PCQ samples, Survival Guide, and Camp Resource Guide contain no references to these terms.

2. **No evidence that specific words like "bet" or "gamble" are flagged** in PCQ reviews. The official documentation describes evaluation criteria (interactivity, visual impact, etc.) but does not mention keyword scanning or word-level filtering.

3. **Decommodification rules prohibit real-money transactions** -- "The sale of products and services by participants is strictly prohibited" and "no cash transactions are permitted between burners" ([Survival Guide 2025](https://survival.burningman.org/rules-and-regulations/commerce-concessions/)). This means real gambling for money would violate event rules, but games using non-monetary stakes (curios, tasks, stories) would not fall under this prohibition.

4. **Casino-themed camps have existed at regional burns** -- Burning Flipside (Texas regional) documented a "Casineaux Camp" using "donated prizes and fake coins." This suggests the concept is accepted within the Burning Man cultural network when properly decommodified.

5. **No placed camp named "Casino" or similar found** in searches of BM theme camp archives for 2018-2025. This does not mean none existed -- the search may simply not have surfaced them. The BM theme camp archive and Playa Events directory were not fully searchable via web search.

6. **The key risk for a casino-themed camp is decommodification perception**, not a specific anti-gambling rule. Reviewers evaluate whether a camp's culture and values align with the 10 Principles. A camp that clearly frames casino activities as decommodified social play (no real money, gifting-based stakes) would need to make this framing explicit and unambiguous in the PCQ to avoid triggering concerns about transactional or commodified experiences.

## Summary: What We Can Verify vs. What We Cannot

### Verified from official sources:
- The 8 criteria names and their linked principles
- Interactivity is the #1 factor for theme camp evaluation
- Concrete, specific language is required (not vague/esoteric)
- First-year camps capped at 100'x100' / 10,000 sq ft
- Camp lead should not be a first-time Burner (strong recommendation)
- Night lighting affects prominent placement
- Frontage must be inviting and draw people in
- Consent/opt-in required for all interactivity
- No transactional exchanges (decommodification)
- MOOP-risky items identified (glitter, confetti, sequins, feathers)
- Post-playa report required for good standing
- Multiple volunteers review each PCQ
- Space demand exceeds supply (competitive for new camps)
- Interactivity must scale proportionally with camp population

### Verified from community sources:
- In-camp activities are what count (not off-playa events)
- Interactivity details must go in the interactivity field specifically
- Enthusiasm and personality are positive signals
- Camp lead must be available and knowledgeable (not a delegated newbie)
- Layout must account for all vehicles, structures, and infrastructure
- First-year camps must justify space with public-facing service

### NOT verified (claims in existing reviewer-criteria.md without sourcing):
- Specific "red flag" and "green flag" language (BM does not use these terms officially; they use "Here's What We Hope To See" and "Here's What We'll Look For")
- Priority ordering of the 8 criteria (official docs do not rank them; they state interactivity is "one of the most important" but don't numerically order all 8)
- Named Safety Lead as a hard requirement (PCQ asks about fire safety liaison for flame effects; general safety lead is not a named PCQ field)
- "Fire extinguishers specified by location" as a PCQ criterion (this appears in the fire safety questionnaire for camps with flame effects, not universally)
- Generator placement "within 20' of street" (Camp Resource Guide recommends generators "just off the street" for fuel truck access, but no 20' rule found)
- Crowd management plan for 250+ (the PCQ asks "Do you anticipate hosting crowds of 250 or more?" but the specific crowd management plan requirement is not detailed in available sources)
- Specific staffing math requirements (person-count x hours x role) -- the PCQ asks for this narrative in "Campers and Interactivity" but no formula or minimum threshold was found

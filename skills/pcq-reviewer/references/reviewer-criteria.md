## The 8 Placement Criteria

Source: [Criteria for Camp Placement & Good Standing](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)

Official page uses two subsections per criterion: "Here's What We Hope To See" and "Here's What We'll Look For." BM does not publicly rank these, but official docs call interactivity "one of the most important factors" for theme camps. Criteria 1–3 apply only to theme camps; 4–8 apply to all placed camps.

### 1. Interactivity
*(Radical Inclusion, Gifting, Communal Effort, Participation)*

**Official guidance:**
- "Take some time to think about what you will offer and write in concrete, descriptive language about the details. Be prepared with a schedule of days and times." — [Completing the PCQ](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024039188)
- "Nebulous, esoteric, or incomplete descriptions leave us confused (and not in a good way)." — same source
- Interactivity must scale proportionally with camp population — [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988)
- In-camp activities count; off-playa/deep-playa events do not — PCQ field instructions + ePlaya community guidance
- "Placement considers how unique your interactivity is" — [Completing the PCQ](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024039188)

**What the PCQ form explicitly asks:**
- Differentiate scheduled vs. spontaneous activities
- Describe as if the reviewer has never seen your camp
- Provide a per-day schedule with times, titles, and participant counts

### 2. Visual Impact
*(Radical Self-expression)*

**Official guidance:**
- "When someone is walking or biking down the street, what will they see? How will people know you're a theme camp? What about your camp would compel them to get off their bike and come participate?" — [Camp Resource Guide](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/so-you-want-to-camp-at-burning-man/camp-resource-guide/)
- "Well-lit camps may be placed in more prominent locations as navigation landmarks." — PCQ Night Lighting field
- "All Esplanade, Portal, Plaza, and Avenue camps must have their frontages open and lit at night through Saturday night (Man Burn)." — PCQ3 placement preferences + Camp Resource Guide

### 3. Uniqueness of Offering
*(Radical Self-expression, Immediacy, Radical Inclusion)*

**Official guidance:**
- "Whether you are offering something that could only be done at Burning Man or doing something standard but in a new and wonderful way" — [Completing the PCQ](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024039188)

### 4. Good Neighbors and Citizens of BRC
*(Radical Inclusion, Civic Responsibility, Communal Effort)*

**Official guidance:**
- Sound plan must comply with BRC Sound Policy or request 2:00/10:00 sector
- Generator noise/exhaust considerations (Camp Resource Guide recommends generators "just off the street" for fuel truck access — no specific distance threshold found in official docs)

### 5. Culture and Values
*(Decommodification, Radical Self-reliance, Radical Inclusion)*

**Official guidance:**
- "The sale of products and services by participants is strictly prohibited." — [Survival Guide](https://survival.burningman.org/rules-and-regulations/commerce-concessions/)
- "No cash transactions are permitted between burners." — same source
- Clear opt-in consent required: "predetermined ways for participants to easily opt in (or out) of any activity" — [Camp Standing Criteria](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)
- No PAYticipation, no concierge arrangements, no convenience camping — [Cultural Direction Setting](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/cultural-direction-setting-decommodification/)

### 6. Safety, Protocols and Procedures
*(Civic Responsibility)*

**Official guidance:**
- PCQ asks whether camp anticipates 250+ crowds (yes/no field)
- Fire safety details required for camps with flame effects (separate questionnaire)
- Fire lanes must be clear on layout

**Not verified as universal requirements** (may apply only to camps with flame effects):
- Fire extinguishers specified by location
- Named Safety Lead
- Generator placement within specific distance of street

### 7. MOOP (Matter Out of Place / Leave No Trace)

**Official guidance:**
- Explicitly flagged MOOP-risk items: glitter (not allowed in BRC), feathers, rhinestones, artificial grass, sequins, synthetic wigs, styrofoam coolers, loose confetti, straw, hay bales — [MOOP page](https://burningman.org/event/preparation/leaving-no-trace/moop/)
- Bad MOOP rating damages camp standing — [Camp Standing Criteria](https://burningman.org/event/camps/camp-standing-criteria/)

### 8. Self-reliance / Strain on Resources
*(Radical Self-reliance)*

**Official guidance:**
- Realistic scope for team size
- Power, water, shade sufficient without external rescue
- Not showing up or not using reserved space damages standing

## First-Year Camp Context

Source: [Planning a Theme Camp](https://help.burningman.org/hc/en-us/articles/4415024099988)

**Official rules:**
- Capped at 100' × 100' / 10,000 sq ft (up to ~25 people)
- First-year camps go through Standard Track (more PCQ fields required)
- Camp lead should not be a first-time Burner (strong recommendation, not hard rule)
- CAMP Advisor recommended for first year

**Competition context:**
- ~1,300 SOI submissions in 2024, including 152 new theme camps — [BM Journal 2024](https://journal.burningman.org/2024/02/black-rock-city/building-brc/adapting-to-growth/)
- ~90% of BRC already placed — limited spots for new camps
- Multiple volunteers review each PCQ — [Placement Team](https://burningman.org/event/participate/camps/placement-team/)

**Community advice (ePlaya):**
- Interactivity details must go in the interactivity field specifically, not elsewhere — [ePlaya 2016](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=76853)
- "Presume the placer knows nothing about your camp" — [ePlaya 2015](https://eplaya.burningman.org/viewtopic.php?t=73380)
- Be specific ("we'll be making amazing yummy quesadillas" not "we're bringing food")
- Enthusiasm and personality are positive signals
- Camp lead must be available by email/phone from submission through placement announcement
- First-year camps must justify space with public-facing service
